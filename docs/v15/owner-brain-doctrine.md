# PlaiceToMeat V15 — The Owner Brain

Status: Master Product Doctrine & Strategic Implementation Specification
Priority: Highest

Supersedes:
- V14.2A TODAY Compression
- Individual operator-guidance improvements
- Future intelligence-layer planning

This document defines the final destination of PlaiceToMeat. Not the next feature. Not the next sprint. The destination. Every future release should move toward this system.

> Roadmap reference: the program that moves the product toward this doctrine is defined in [00-roadmap-v14.3-to-v15.5.md](00-roadmap-v14.3-to-v15.5.md).

---

## The Fundamental Realisation

Most butcher software helps record what happened.
PlaiceToMeat must help decide what to do next.
That is the entire moat.

---

## The Product Mission

The butcher should never need to think about:
- inventory systems
- purchasing systems
- stock control
- forecasting
- waste reduction
- margin analysis
- operational prioritisation

The software does the thinking. The butcher does the doing.

---

## The Core Formula

Traditional software: Data → Dashboard → Interpretation → Decision → Action
PlaiceToMeat: **Data → Decision → Action**

Interpretation disappears.

---

## The Ultimate Rule

Every release must satisfy: **maximum system sophistication, minimum operator skill required.**
If the system becomes more powerful, the operator experience must become *simpler* — never more complicated.

---

## What PlaiceToMeat Becomes

Not POS software, inventory software, butcher ERP, or a management dashboard.
It becomes an operational co-pilot. An owner brain. A second manager.

---

## The Owner Brain Model

The software continuously answers — without the butcher needing to ask:
- What needs attention?
- What should be sold?
- What should be ordered?
- What should be counted?
- What should be fixed?
- What should be ignored?

---

## The Three-Layer Architecture

**Layer 1 — Reality Engine (invisible).** Inventory truth, movement ledger, FEFO, reconciliation, confidence, stock/purchasing/waste/operational history. The butcher never sees this layer.

**Layer 2 — Decision Engine (invisible).** Prioritisation, ranking, action generation, risk/opportunity/confidence evaluation. The butcher never sees this layer.

**Layer 3 — Operator Layer (visible).** Actions, recommendations, guidance. Nothing else.

---

## The Death Of Dashboards

Most business software fails by providing charts, graphs, metrics, and reports — forcing humans to think. PlaiceToMeat should aggressively avoid this.

**Dashboard Rule:** a metric may only exist if it changes behaviour. Otherwise remove it.

---

## TODAY Becomes The Business

TODAY is no longer a dashboard. TODAY becomes the business itself.
The butcher opens TODAY. The software already knows everything else.

---

## The Three Action Rule

Never present more than 3 primary actions. Ever.

Examples:
- Sell Lamb Leg first.
- Count Chicken Breast today.
- Order Chicken tomorrow.

Done.

---

## Action Compression Engine

The software may process 10,000 stock movements, 500 products, 100 batches, 50 warnings.
The butcher receives **3 actions.**

---

## Action Ranking Formula

Every action competes against every other action:
- Which action prevents the most waste?
- Which action protects the most money?
- Which action prevents the biggest problem?
- Which action matters today?

Only winners appear.

---

## The "Don't Make Me Think" Rule

The butcher should never calculate days of stock, stock velocity, confidence, waste percentage, margin trends, or expiry ranking. The software calculates. The butcher acts.

---

## Operating Philosophies

- **Inventory.** The butcher does not manage inventory. The software manages inventory. The butcher handles meat.
- **Purchasing.** The butcher does not forecast demand. The software forecasts demand. The butcher places the order.
- **Waste.** The butcher does not analyse waste. The software analyses waste. The butcher follows guidance.
- **Confidence.** The butcher never sees confidence scores. Never.
- **Product health.** Not "Inventory health score: 74". Instead "Needs Attention".

---

## Intelligence Hierarchy

The software must continuously rank, in this order:
1. Prevent loss.
2. Prevent waste.
3. Prevent stock-outs.
4. Protect sales.
5. Reduce work.
6. Improve profit.

---

## The Time Rules

- **One-Minute Rule.** A new butcher understands what matters today within one minute, without training.
- **Ten-Second Rule.** An experienced butcher knows what to do next within ten seconds.

---

## Language Doctrine

The software speaks like an experienced butcher. Never ERP, accounting, software, or analytics language.

| Bad | Good |
|---|---|
| Inventory confidence degraded. | Please count this item today. |
| Forecasted stock exhaustion. | Order tomorrow. |
| High variance product. | Stock keeps changing unexpectedly. |

---

## The Calm System Principle

The software never panics, never overwhelms, never floods the operator.
Not "17 warnings, 9 alerts, 4 errors, 3 notices." Instead: **three actions. Everything else waits.**

---

## The Operator Stress Test

Assume: customer waiting, gloves on, interrupted, tired, busy, phone ringing.
If the workflow fails under those conditions, redesign it.

---

## The Future Roadmap

Only **after** this doctrine is achieved should the following be considered: one-tap actions, smart bundles, dynamic markdowns, waste coaching, margin intelligence, daily owner briefing, supplier intelligence, customer intelligence, SMS, WhatsApp. All future intelligence must obey this doctrine.

---

## The Final Success Metric

Success is not "this software is powerful", "has many features", or "is technically impressive".
Success is the butcher saying: **"I always know what to do."** — without understanding why.

At that moment PlaiceToMeat stops being software. It becomes an operational advantage.
