# Carcass intake

Breaking a whole or half animal into saleable cuts, pricing each one, and putting
them into stock.

## Why it matters
This is where most of your margin is made or lost. The cut sheet estimates the yield;
reality decides your real cost per kg.

## Step by step
1. Weigh the whole carcass and record it against the intake.
2. Use the cutting guide to see the expected cuts, yields and suggested prices.
3. Cut, then weigh each saleable cut and the bone/fat/trim separately.
4. Confirm the real weights in the intake — this is what teaches the shop your true yield.
5. Review the suggested prices, adjust if you want, then commit the cuts to stock.

## Watch out for
- Always confirm actual weights. The "expected vs actual" learning only works if you do.
- If actual yield is regularly below the estimate, your suggested prices are too low — the briefing will tell you.
