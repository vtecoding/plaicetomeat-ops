# Receiving stock

Booking in meat as it arrives from a supplier so the shop knows what you have, what
it cost, and when it must sell by.

## Why it matters
Every stock, cost and ordering figure starts here. Sloppy receiving means every
number downstream is wrong.

## Step by step
1. Check the delivery against the invoice — right products, right weights, right price.
2. Look at it: colour, smell, packaging intact, temperature cold to the touch.
3. Record the batch in Stock: product, weight received, cost, supplier, and the use-by date.
4. Note the country of origin and halal certificate reference if the supplier provides one.
5. Put it away fast — chiller for fresh, freezer for frozen — oldest stock at the front.

## Watch out for
- Don't accept meat that's warm, off-colour or past date — refuse it and tell the supplier.
- Enter the real weight, not the ordered weight. They're often different.
