# Butcher words explained

A plain-words guide to the butchery and shop terms you'll see around the app. No
prior experience needed.

## Why it matters
The app uses a few trade words. Knowing what they mean — especially yield and
margin — lets you make confident decisions instead of guessing.

## The words
1. **Carcass** — a whole animal after slaughter, before it's cut up. You buy these and break them into cuts.
2. **Cut (or joint)** — a piece of meat ready to sell, like a leg, shoulder or chops.
3. **Yield** — how much saleable meat you actually get from a carcass after bone, fat and trim come off. More yield means more to sell.
4. **Trim** — the fat, sinew and offcuts removed while preparing a cut. Usually goes to mince or waste.
5. **Bone-in / boneless** — whether a cut still has its bone. Boneless weighs less but is often easier to sell.
6. **Batch** — one delivery or carcass recorded in the shop, with its own weight, cost and use-by date.
7. **Cost per kg** — what the meat costs you to buy, per kilo. The app needs this to work out your profit.
8. **Margin** — the profit left after costs, shown as a share of the price. A bigger margin is a healthier line.
9. **Days of cover** — how many days your current stock will last at the rate it's selling.
10. **Cutting guide / cut sheet** — the plan for how a carcass breaks into cuts, with expected weights and suggested prices.
11. **Use-by (expiry)** — the date meat must be sold or used by. After it, treat it as waste.
12. **Halal certificate** — a supplier's proof their meat is halal. It must be kept in date.

## Watch out for
- You don't need to memorise these — come back whenever a word on screen is unfamiliar.
- The two that drive your profit most are yield and margin. Get comfortable with those first.
