# Recording waste

Logging anything you throw away — spoiled stock, trim, or a customer return.

## Why it matters
Waste is money leaving the shop. If you don't log it, you can't see which products
lose money or how much you're over-ordering.

## Step by step
1. When something is thrown away, open Stock and record a waste event.
2. Pick the product, the weight, and the reason (expired, damaged, trim, customer issue).
3. Do it the moment it happens, while you remember the real amount.
4. Review the weekly briefing to see your biggest waste source.

## Watch out for
- Trim from butchering is normal — but logging it shows the true cost of a cut.
- A product wasted week after week usually means you're buying more than you sell.
