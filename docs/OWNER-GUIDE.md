# PlaiceToMeat — Owner's Guide

A plain-English guide to running the shop from your phone, tablet or computer.
No computer knowledge needed. If anything here doesn't match what you see on
screen, call your support person — don't guess.

---

## The two screens you'll use

You only ever need two screens. Everything else is just detail behind these.

| Screen | Who uses it | What it's for |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| **Counter** | You and counter staff | See today's orders and move them along as you prepare them |
| **Owner Dashboard** | You | Your morning overview — money, stock, what needs doing |

You sign in once with **Staff login** (top right). Staff see the Counter.
You, as the owner, also see the Owner Dashboard.

---

## Every morning (2 minutes)

Open the **Owner Dashboard**. Read it top to bottom like a newspaper:

1. **Good Morning box** — orders waiting, stock going off today, certificates
   running out, yesterday's takings and waste, and your best-selling product.
2. **Today's Actions** — a short to-do list written in plain English, built from
   your real shop figures. Do the **Urgent** ones first. This is also where you'll
   see the **big-day reminders** (see below).
3. **Opening / Closing checklists** — tick them off as you go.

That's your whole morning briefing on one screen.

## Taking and preparing orders (the Counter screen)

Customers order online and pay when they collect. Orders appear on the **Counter**
screen by themselves — you don't refresh anything.

Orders move left to right through four columns:

```
  Incoming  →  Prepping  →  Ready  →  Collected
```

- A new order lands in **Incoming**.
- Tap **Start Prep** when you begin cutting/packing it → it moves to **Prepping**.
- Tap **Mark Ready** when it's bagged and waiting → it moves to **Ready**
  (this is when the customer gets their "your order is ready" text, if texts are on).
- Tap **Collected** when they've picked it up and paid → it moves to **Collected**.

Each order card shows the customer's name, phone number (tap to call), what they
ordered, the collection time, and how urgent it is (cards turn **amber** then
**red** as the collection time approaches).

**Staff notes:** you can add a private note to any order (e.g. "regular — likes it
trimmed"). Customers never see these.

### The little status line at the top

- **Green "Live — new orders appear on their own"** = all good, orders come through
  automatically.
- **Amber "Checking for new orders every 15s"** = still working fine, just checking
  on a timer instead of instantly. Orders still appear.
- **Red "Not updating on its own — tap Refresh"** = tap the **Refresh** button to
  pull the latest orders. Everything still works; it just won't auto-update.

If in doubt, tap **Refresh**. You can't break anything.

## Changing what you sell — products and prices

Owner Dashboard → **Products & Prices**.

- Add a new product, change a price, or mark something as out of stock — all
  yourself, no support needed.
- Prices are always taken from here, so a customer can never be charged the wrong
  amount.

Set it up once before launch, then just keep it tidy (new lines, price changes,
sold-out toggles).

## When customers can collect — and days you're closed

- **Collection Times** — the time slots customers can choose when they order.
  Turn a slot off and it disappears from the website immediately.
- **Closed Days** — holidays and days you're shut. Customers can't book those days.

## Halal & food-safety paperwork

Owner Dashboard → **Supplier Certificates**. Record each supplier's halal /
food-safety certificate and its expiry date. The dashboard warns you **30 days
before** one runs out so you're never caught short. Keeping this current is what
backs up your halal promise to customers.

## Stock & waste (optional but worth it)

Owner Dashboard → **Stock & Waste**. If you log what you take in and what you bin,
the dashboard will:

- warn you what's **going off soon** so you can use or discount it,
- show you **where money's being lost** to waste,
- estimate your **daily profit**, and
- tell you when you're about to **run low** on a popular line.

You don't have to use it — orders and the counter work without it — but it's where
the real money-saving insight comes from.

## The big-day reminders (your biggest earner)

The dashboard automatically counts down to the days that make a butcher's year and
tells you to prepare in good time:

- **Eid al-Adha (Qurbani)** — the biggest day of all. You'll be reminded **5 weeks
  ahead** to take pre-orders and book whole-lamb/goat supply.
- **Eid al-Fitr** — reminded ~4 weeks ahead.
- **Ramadan** — reminded ahead of the month of rising evening demand.
- **Christmas & New Year** — reminded ~3 weeks ahead for joints and turkey.
- **Easter and the BBQ bank holidays** — reminded ~1 week ahead.

Islamic dates depend on the moon sighting, so the app shows them as **estimated** —
always confirm the exact day with your community. The point of the reminder is to
give you enough notice to order extra and not miss the rush.

## When something looks off

| You see | What it means | What to do |
|---------|---------------|------------|
| "Texts that didn't send" has a number | A customer "ready" text didn't go | Phone that customer. Orders are unaffected. |
| Counter screen says red / not updating | Auto-updates dropped | Tap **Refresh**. Keep serving as normal. |
| "Practice orders today" has a number | Someone placed a training/test order | Normal during setup. Not counted in takings. |
| A price looks wrong online | Product price needs editing | Owner Dashboard → Products & Prices. |

**Golden rule:** taking orders, preparing them and collecting payment work even if
texts are off or the screen isn't auto-updating. Nothing here can lose an order.

## Before you open: the Launch Readiness panel

At the very top of your Owner Dashboard is a **Launch Readiness** panel. It checks
the things that must be in place before opening — products, prices, collection
times, supplier certificates, a test order, a staff account — and shows one of:

- **Ready to open** — every check it can see is done.
- **Needs attention** — something's missing; the panel says exactly what.
- **Not started** — the shop hasn't been set up yet.

It's honest: it only ticks what it can actually verify. Two items ("owner login
reviewed" and "customer pages reviewed") are for you to confirm yourself — the app
won't pretend it can check those for you. Work alongside the full
**GO-LIVE-CHECKLIST**.

## Who does what — the four levels of access

| Role | Can do | Cannot do |
|------|--------|-----------|
| **Owner (you/your dad)** | Everything, including the technical "System Checks" and "Activity History" pages | — |
| **Manager** | Run the shop: orders, products, prices, stock, certificates, settings, reports | Reach the System Checks or Activity History pages |
| **Counter staff** | View and move orders (Start Prep, Mark Ready, Collected), add staff notes | Change prices, edit products or certificates, change settings, or open any admin page |
| **Customer** | Browse the shop, order, and track their own order | See anything staff-only |

Give counter staff their **own** login — never share the owner password. Dangerous
actions (changing prices, deleting products, editing certificates) are simply not
available to counter staff, by design.

- **Support person:** anything technical, the "System Checks" page, and anything in
  this guide that doesn't match your screen.
